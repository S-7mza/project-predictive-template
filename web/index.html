Audience

  • Managers: see status, decisions, and gates.
  • Business owners: understand workshops and benefits.
  • Engineers: jump into data, ETL, and QA.

How to use

  1. Start with Overview to align on context and share the link with your core team and sponsor.
  2. Use Timeline & Workshops as your master calendar: confirm dates, participants, and use the Docs links to open pre-work, agendas, and comms plans for each session.
  3. Before any gate or major decision, open PMI & Governance to check which artifacts, risks, and decisions must be updated.
  4. During delivery, keep Agile Delivery as your sprint hub: write stories, update backlog and trackers, and link work to KPIs.
  5. Use Artifacts Library as the single source of truth for templates, logs, and documentation referenced in meetings and emails.
  6. Open Audit to review process scores against PMI, ISO, Agile, and maturity references, and follow the suggested improvements to move closer to 10/10 on each dimension.

Gates & decisions

Before moving between major phases (Discovery → Modeling → ETL → Prototype → UAT → Deployment), run a gate review using the gating checklist.

  • Ensure required artifacts exist and are up-to-date.
  • Capture Go / No-Go / Conditional decisions.
  • Log actions in the Action Log with owners and due dates.
